Research Profile

Survey Methodology, Machine Learning, and Computational Social Science

My research focuses on how data is generated, measured, and modeled in complex social systems. I work at the intersection of survey methodology, NLP, LLMs, and data-centric machine learning, with an emphasis on measurement error, bias, uncertainty, and inference from human-generated data.

Current Research

FrameScope: AI Metaphor and Framing Analysis

LLMs + NLP + Computational Social Science

FrameScope studies how artificial intelligence is framed in public discourse across Reddit, news, and other online sources. The project uses LLM-assisted annotation, metaphor detection, stance classification, and dashboard-based visualization to analyze how people understand AI as a tool, threat, replacement, assistant, machine, force, or social actor.

Research Themes

Data Generation & Measurement

Methods that account for data quality, missingness, bias, uncertainty, and the structure of data-generating processes.

Survey + AI Systems

Research Philosophy

Many failures in machine learning and computational social science are not only model failures; they are failures to understand the data-generating process. My research focuses on building methods that treat data quality, measurement, sampling, and uncertainty as central parts of inference rather than downstream technical details.
