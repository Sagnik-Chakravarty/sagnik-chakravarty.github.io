System Design

How SagnikGPT Was Built

A retrieval-augmented portfolio assistant built to answer questions from my resume, research papers, posters, notes, and project materials.

SagnikGPT retrieves relevant chunks from a portfolio knowledge base and uses an LLM backend to synthesize grounded answers. Instead of relying only on general model knowledge, it answers using project-specific materials such as papers, posters, resume entries, and documentation.

1. Knowledge Base

Resume, papers, posters, transcripts, project notes, and supporting files.

2. Retrieval Layer

Documents are chunked, embedded, and searched through semantic retrieval.

3. LLM Backend

A FastAPI backend retrieves relevant context and generates responses through an LLM.

4. Web Interface

The assistant is embedded directly into the portfolio with session memory and markdown rendering.

Tech stack: HTML/CSS/JavaScript, FastAPI, ChromaDB, Groq, Llama, Python, PDF/CSV/TXT ingestion, OCR-assisted poster parsing.

Research Agenda

The methodological throughline behind my work

Rather than listing broad research areas again, this section explains the deeper methodological questions connecting my projects across surveys, NLP, LLMs, public discourse, and machine learning.

01

Data as a measurement process

I treat survey responses, social media posts, news articles, and LLM annotations as outputs of measurement systems rather than neutral data points. This connects my work on ASHA response behavior, immigration narratives, AI discourse, and public sentiment.

Ask SagnikGPT →
02

Survey methodology as infrastructure for AI

My survey training gives structure to problems that ML systems often ignore: who is represented, who is missing, how labels are generated, how measurement error enters, and how uncertainty should be carried into downstream inference.

Ask SagnikGPT →
03

Narratives as social indicators

Projects such as FrameScope, Immigration Narratives, GTI/media framing, and EV sentiment all ask how public narratives can be measured, compared, and interpreted without treating them as direct substitutes for reality.

Ask SagnikGPT →
04

Research systems, not just static analyses

I am increasingly interested in building reusable systems: dashboards, retrieval-augmented assistants, annotation workflows, and reproducible pipelines that make research outputs easier to inspect, update, and communicate.

Research Philosophy

Better models require better understanding of data generation.

Many failures in machine learning and computational social science are not only model failures; they are failures to understand the data-generating process. My research treats data quality, measurement, sampling, and uncertainty as central parts of inference.
