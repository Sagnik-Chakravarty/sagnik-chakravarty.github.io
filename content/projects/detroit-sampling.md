## Summary
Designed a multistage area-frame survey for transportation insecurity measurement in Detroit.

## Objective
Create a probability sample design capable of estimating transportation insecurity across adult age domains.

## Abstract
The design uses census tracts as PSUs, block groups as SSUs, and persons as final sampling units, with domain targets and response-rate inflation.

## Motivation
Transportation insecurity is unevenly distributed, requiring a design that accounts for geography, age domains, cost, and response heterogeneity.

## Methods
Multistage area-frame sampling, PPS logic, tract/block group frame construction, response-rate inflation, domain allocation, inclusion probability, and weighting logic.

## Results
Produced an implementable multistage design with selected PSUs, block group frame, target completes, and weighting structure.

## Insight
Shows applied survey design ability: translating a policy measurement problem into a probability sampling plan.
