## Summary
Designed a statewide multistage school-based sampling plan for teen smoking and drug-use estimation.

## Objective
Estimate teen risk behaviors using a cost-efficient school-based probability design.

## Abstract
This applied sampling project developed a stratified multistage school-based design with cluster allocation, design effect reasoning, and representativeness goals.

## Motivation
Youth risk surveys require efficient school-based sampling while preserving regional and subgroup representation.

## Methods
Stratification, multistage cluster design, PPS logic, sample allocation, design effect assessment, and survey operations planning.

## Results
Recommended a practical sample design balancing cost, precision, and statewide representativeness.

## Insight
Demonstrates core sampling training from target population to frame, stages, allocation, and inference.
