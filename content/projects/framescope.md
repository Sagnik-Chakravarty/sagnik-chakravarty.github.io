## Summary
A live LLM-assisted system for metaphor, stance, tone, and framing analysis in AI discourse.

## Objective
Build an interpretable pipeline for measuring how AI is framed in public discourse.

## Abstract
FrameScope studies AI discourse by extracting metaphor categories, stance, tone, and framing signals from social and media data, then exposing the outputs through a live dashboard.

## Motivation
Public discussions of AI are shaped by recurring metaphors such as tool, threat, replacement, machine, child, or force.

## Methods
LLM-assisted annotation, metaphor category coding, stance classification, tone estimation, dashboarding, and social/media data processing.

## Results
Produced a recruiter-visible research system: GitHub repository, live dashboard, paper, preliminary slides, and reusable annotation pipeline.

## Insight
Demonstrates applied NLP engineering, computational social science, and research-product development.
