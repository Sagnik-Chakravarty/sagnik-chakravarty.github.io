## Summary
Analyzed stance and sentiment around U.S.-Ukraine discourse before and after a political event.

## Objective
Measure how a major political event shifted social media sentiment and stance toward the U.S. and Ukraine.

## Abstract
The project compared traditional machine-learning classifiers and LLM-based classification for event-centered political stance detection.

## Motivation
Political discourse shifts rapidly after visible diplomatic events, but stance categories can be subtle and imbalanced.

## Methods
Event-window analysis, hand coding, SVM, decision trees, Word2Vec features, and LLM few-shot stance evaluation.

## Results
Traditional ML struggled with class imbalance; LLM prompting provided better qualitative leverage for nuanced stance categories.

## Insight
Hybrid NLP evaluation is valuable when political labels are sparse, ambiguous, and context-sensitive.
