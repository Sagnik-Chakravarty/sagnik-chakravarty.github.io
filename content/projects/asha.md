## Summary
Evaluated postcard reminders and survey-mode effects for ASHA survey response improvement.

## Objective
Estimate whether postcard reminders and survey mode decisions improve response behavior in ASHA surveys.

## Abstract
The project evaluated a healthcare postcard reminder experiment and mode effects using policy estimands, bootstrap inference, subgroup response comparisons, and logistic models.

## Motivation
Declining response rates threaten survey inference quality, and naive comparison of reminder groups can be biased by sequential nonresponse.

## Methods
Policy-level response estimands, conditional probability decomposition, stratified bootstrap, chi-square tests, logistic regression, and subgroup mode comparison.

## Results
The first postcard produced a modest response-rate increase; the second postcard added little incremental benefit.

## Insight
Operational recommendation: prioritize the first postcard and evaluate repeated follow-up against cost.
