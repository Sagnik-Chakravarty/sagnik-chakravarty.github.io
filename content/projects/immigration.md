## Summary
Compared news and Reddit narratives with CBP and ICE administrative enforcement indicators.

## Objective
Assess whether narrative indicators track or diverge from administrative immigration enforcement data.

## Abstract
The project treats narrative data as a measurement process and compares GDELT/news and Reddit indicators against CBP and ICE enforcement series.

## Motivation
Media and social narratives are often used as proxies for real-world events, but they can contain coverage, tone, selection, and timing errors.

## Methods
Monthly aggregation, standardization, time-series alignment, correlations, lagged comparison, regression overlays, and Total Survey Error framing.

## Results
Narrative indicators showed partial association with enforcement data but meaningful divergence and timing differences.

## Insight
Narratives should be treated as noisy measurements, not direct substitutes for administrative records.
