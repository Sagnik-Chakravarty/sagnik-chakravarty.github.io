## Summary
Modeled football player market value using cross-classified multilevel models.

## Objective
Estimate how player characteristics, team context, time, and transfers shape market valuation.

## Abstract
The project used player-season data and hierarchical modeling to account for repeated observations, club contexts, transfers, and player-level heterogeneity.

## Motivation
Many player valuation models ignore crossed player-club structures and the fact that players transfer across teams over time.

## Methods
Cross-classified multilevel modeling, REML estimation, polynomial time trends, player and club random effects, market value modeling.

## Results
Player-level heterogeneity explained most valuation variance; minutes played emerged as a strong positive valuation signal.

## Insight
Sports valuation models require hierarchical structure; player quality dominates but team and time context still matter.
