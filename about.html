About Me

Hi! My name is Sagnik. I am a graduate student in the Joint Program in Survey Methodology (JPSM) at the University of Maryland, College Park. I currently work as a research assistant in the CLIP/CATS lab with Julia Mendelsohn, where I study how people talk about artificial intelligence using computational methods.

My research interests lie at the intersection of survey methodology, machine learning, and computational social science. I work on problems related to measurement error, sampling, nonresponse, and the use of large language models for tasks such as annotation, framing analysis, and survey-based inference.

More broadly, I am interested in how machine learning systems can better account for how data is actually generated, including issues like bias, uncertainty, coverage, and missingness.

I received my MS in Data Science from Vellore Institute of Technology and my BS in Statistics from Amity University, Kolkata. I have also worked as a data science research intern at the Indian Statistical Institute.
