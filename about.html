Research Identity

From statistics to data science to survey methodology

My academic path began in statistics, moved through applied data science, and now centers on survey methodology and data-centric AI. Across these stages, the core question has stayed the same: how do we make reliable inferences from imperfect, human-generated data?

At UMD, I work as a research assistant in the CLIP/CATS research environment with Julia Mendelsohn, studying how people talk about artificial intelligence using computational text methods. This includes metaphor detection, framing analysis, stance classification, and LLM-assisted annotation.

I am especially interested in the idea that many machine learning failures are not simply model failures. They are often failures to understand data generation, measurement error, coverage, nonresponse, sampling, and uncertainty.

Research Philosophy

Better models require better understanding of data generation.

I want to build methods and systems that do not treat data as a fixed input. Instead, my work asks how measurement, coverage, sampling, nonresponse, missingness, annotation, and human behavior shape the data before any model sees it.
