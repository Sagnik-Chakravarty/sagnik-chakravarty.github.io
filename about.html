About Me

I am a graduate student at the University of Maryland, College Park in the Joint Program in Survey Methodology, specializing in Survey and Data Science. My work focuses on computational social science, survey methodology, machine learning, and NLP applications for social data.

My current research centers on data-centric machine learning, measurement error, sampling, nonresponse, and the use of large language models to improve survey research and computational social science workflows.

I am particularly interested in how machine learning systems can incorporate the realities of data generation — including uncertainty, bias, coverage, and missingness — into modeling and inference.

I am actively exploring opportunities in survey research, data science, machine learning, NLP, and computational social science.
